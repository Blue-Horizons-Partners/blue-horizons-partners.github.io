## Image Optimization Guidelines

### General Best Practices
1. **Compress all images** before uploading
   - Use tools like TinyPNG, ImageOptim, or Squoosh
   - Aim for < 200KB for most images
   - Hero images can be up to 500KB

2. **Use appropriate formats**
   - **JPG** for photographs
   - **PNG** for logos, icons, graphics with transparency
   - **WebP** for modern browsers (optional, provide JPG fallback)
   - **SVG** for simple graphics/logos (smallest file size)

3. **Responsive images**
   - Create multiple sizes for key images (hero, headers)
   - Prevents loading huge images on mobile devices

4. **Alt text**
   - Always provide descriptive alt text
   - Improves accessibility and SEO

5. **File naming**
   - Use lowercase
   - Use hyphens, not spaces or underscores
   - Be descriptive: `about-team-office.jpg` not `img001.jpg`

## Notes on Color Scheme

Consider your images working with these potential theme colors:
- **Default skin:** White background, gray text, blue accents
- **Air skin:** Blues and light colors
- **Contrast skin:** White/dark with bold blue links
- **Professional recommendation:** Keep images with neutral tones that work across skins

Ensure hero/header images have enough contrast for white text overlay.

## Questions to Consider

Before creating/sourcing images:

1. **Branding:** Do you have existing brand colors/style guide to follow?
2. **Tone:** What feeling should images convey? (professional, innovative, approachable, corporate)
3. **People:** Should images include people? Diverse representation?
4. **Location:** Should images show your office, your city, or be more abstract?
5. **Consistency:** Will you use photography, illustrations, or a mix?
